# Hidden Worlds Phase Map

## Protected Core

Every Person Lives in a World You Cannot See.

Any feature that makes the app feel certain, diagnostic, therapeutic, gamified, or narrowly school/work focused should be removed or rewritten.

## Assumptions

- Hidden Worlds is separate from the Tone family, but should feel like it belongs in the same world: dark, quiet, instrument-like, restrained, and emotionally precise.
- The product should be useful for work, family, love, friendship, money, body, culture, grief, digital life, public space, strangers, and change.
- The app should not claim to know why someone behaved a certain way. It should offer possible worlds and better questions.
- The web app is the current live product surface. iOS can follow the same IA and visual system.

## Completed Pass

1. Tone reference extraction: use Tone Recall's ritual pacing, dark field, soft glow, and chamber depth without copying its waveform/dial language.
2. IA simplification: make the main loop Lens -> Atlas -> Daily.
3. Feature elimination: remove top-level Swap; fold "same moment, many worlds" into Lens results through alternate possible worlds.
4. Atlas positioning: present the 112-entry library as the comprehensive searchable World Atlas.
5. Safety copy: reduce certainty language, avoid clinical/student/productivity drift, and add stronger guidance for health or unsafe situations.
6. Aesthetic pass: make the path/door mark the Hidden Worlds signature and reduce dashboard-like panels.

## Premium Loop 2026-07-08

1. Product coherence: re-centered the app on the protected core. The app now says "consider" instead of "reveal" so it does not imply hidden-truth detection.
2. First-run UX: removed the app overview prelude from the working app surface. Lens is now the first real screen.
3. Information architecture: kept the spine to three modes: Lens, Atlas, Daily. Reflection Room is a chamber, not a fourth destination.
4. Library depth: expanded explicit metadata, alternate worlds, aliases, domains, lenses, and boundaries for high-visibility atlas entries. The full atlas remains 112 entries.
5. Search model: normalized older and newer entries through runtime fallbacks so every world is searchable by behavior, need, life room, tags, and possible context.
6. Reflection Room: added a full detail layer with surface, possible world, possible protection, better question, what not to assume, adjacent prompts, and actions back into Lens or Atlas.
7. Atlas scan quality: changed the Atlas from full cards to compact door rows so 112 worlds feel like a map, not a feed.
8. Daily ritual: added three quiet actions: Open reflection, Try in Lens, and Find in Atlas. No streaks, rewards, or pressure.
9. Safety hardening: strengthened emergency, crisis, legal, therapy, medical, and younger-user guardrails. Health and authority/power entries now prioritize qualified help and real-world safety.
10. Rendered QA: verified mobile and desktop app flows with Playwright screenshots, console checks, modal focus checks, overflow checks, Atlas search, Daily-to-Lens, and landing-page availability copy.

## Assumptions Made Autonomously

- The web app is the immediate product surface to bring to premium level before iOS parity.
- "Comprehensive" should mean broad, searchable, humane coverage rather than a bloated exercise library.
- The portal/path is the Hidden Worlds instrument. It should carry the aesthetic role that the orb/wave carries in Tone.
- Safety language should be more explicit even when it slightly interrupts the contemplative mood.

## Self-Critique After Premium Loop

- The app is much more coherent, but the remaining 56 atlas entries should eventually receive custom alternate worlds and boundary copy instead of relying on fallbacks.
- The Reflection Room is now strong, but future versions should add a saved private reflection model only if it stays local-first and non-addictive.
- The static Lens is philosophically safer than AI generation, but the future AI layer will need evals that punish certainty, diagnosis, persuasion, and excuse-making.
- iOS parity is still not complete in this pass. The SwiftUI app should reuse this IA and object language rather than invent a separate experience.

## Next Phases

1. Atlas normalization: add rich metadata to every older atlas entry, not only the newest forty.
2. Deep entry view: give each atlas world a fuller reflection page with surface, possible world, what not to assume, boundary, better question, and adjacent prompts.
3. Daily refinement: make Daily feel sacred and minimal, with no streaks or tracking.
4. iOS parity: port the Lens -> Atlas -> Daily structure and Tone-family aesthetic into SwiftUI.
5. AI layer: generate possibilities with strict humility, never diagnosis or certainty.
6. QA and review: run red-team checks from investor, App Store, therapist, educator, parent, and teen perspectives before each major release.

## Current Critique

- The app is now clearer, but the Atlas still needs richer metadata across all 112 worlds to feel truly comprehensive.
- The web app has a stronger instrument feel, but the iOS version still needs a dedicated pass to match this direction.
- The current static Lens is safe and fast, but a future AI layer will need rigorous prompt and evaluation guardrails before it should ship.
