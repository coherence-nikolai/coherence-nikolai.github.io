Nikolai

A short introduction.

I’m Nikolai, founder of Coherence Nikolai and creator of these apps.

Coherence Nikolai is a collection of private apps for meditation practice, emotional steadiness, study support, and self-inquiry.

No ads. No social feed. No account where possible. The aim is simple: useful support when you need it.

Tone app family

State, reaction, ritual, reflection, and focus.

mind state Tone Recall Choose and revisit a mind state. Select a state, choose a sound, save it, and return to it later. Best for: preparing for stress before it takes over. Private by design before reaction Tone Steady Pause before reacting. Name the state, steady the body, and choose a better response before speaking or acting. Best for: being caught in a reaction and needing a pause. daily practice Tone Ritual Turn intention into practice. Choose a ritual, receive small actions, and save a short note. Best for: making a state easier to practise each day. reflection prompts Tone Threshold Use prompts for transition points. Move through reflective prompts when you are between states or decisions. Best for: moments when you need to pause and reflect. private journal Tone Mirror Reflect with a symbol and a note. Choose a symbol, breathe with it, write a reflection, and save it privately. Best for: symbolic reflection and private journaling. Private by design visual focus Tone Glyph Use geometry as a focus object. Use a geometric pattern for breath, focus, and personal glyph creation. Best for: visual attention and symbolic practice. opposite states Tone Paradox Work with two opposite states. Place a state and its opposite in one visual field. Best for: practising with contradiction and inner polarity. daily focus Tone Compass Choose a direction for the day. Use a private compass for daily prompts, practices, and reflection. Best for: choosing a simple focus for the day. reflection tool Tone Loom Build a symbolic practice path. Use a browser tool for tone-based reflection and personal mapping. Best for: symbolic reflection and personal meaning-making.

Sota practice family

Meditation, noting, insight practice, and self-inquiry.

practice guide Sota Structure for meditation practice. Time sits, track practice, use insight maps, and review your practice history. Best for: insight practitioners who want structure. insight practice Unfabricate Examine what is happening now. Notice contact, feeling, reaction, release, and the next helpful action. Best for: direct practice with perception and release. haiku practice Sota Haiku Short practice with one poem. Choose a poem, listen, and sit with what is already happening. Best for: short practice when a full sit feels too much. self-inquiry I-Sense Investigate the felt sense of self. Observe location, texture, ownership, agency, observer, boundary, and change. Best for: investigating the felt sense of “I am” without adopting a doctrine.
